```markdown
The goal of a product summary is not to replace the full brochure or policy contract — it’s to help agents quickly answer 3 questions:

1. Who is this product for?
2. When should I recommend it?
3. Why this product instead of another one?

A good internal product summary should help agents:

* shortlist products fast
* position products confidently
* avoid compliance mistakes
* compare similar plans easily
* remember key selling points

⸻

Recommended Product Summary Structure

The best format is usually:

* 1 page
* highly scannable
* consistent template across all products
* simple enough for junior agents
* detailed enough for experienced advisers

⸻

Ideal Product Summary Sections

1. Product Snapshot (Top Section)

This is the “elevator pitch.”

Item    Purpose
Product name    Quick identification
Product type    Term / whole life / ILP / endowment / CI etc
Insurance company   Insurer reference
Target segment  Young adults / families / HNW / retirees
Main objective  Protection / savings / legacy / retirement
Key value proposition   Why this product exists

Example:

ABC Life SecureTerm
Type: Level Term
Target: Young families
Purpose: High affordable protection
Main USP: High coverage at low premium with flexible CI riders

⸻

2. Ideal Client Profile

This is probably the MOST useful section for agents.

Include:

* Typical age group
* Income profile
* Family situation
* Financial goals
* Suitable risk appetite

Example:

Suitable For    Not Ideal For
Young parents   Clients wanting cash value
Mortgage protection Legacy planning
Budget-conscious clients    Wealth accumulation

This helps agents instantly know when to use the product.

⸻

3. Core Features

Only include features that materially affect recommendation.

Examples:

* Coverage duration
* Renewable?
* Convertible?
* Participating/non-participating
* Guaranteed cash value?
* Premium structure
* Riders available
* Multiplier benefits
* CI stages covered
* Premium waiver options

Avoid copying brochure wording.

Instead of:

“Comprehensive holistic financial protection solution”

Use:

“Includes early, intermediate, and late CI coverage.”

⸻

4. Key Selling Points (USPs)

This is the sales positioning section.

Limit to:

* 3–5 strongest differentiators

Examples:

* Competitive premiums for smokers
* Strong ECI definitions
* High multiplier years
* Guaranteed insurability option
* Flexible premium holidays
* Strong surrender values
* High overseas coverage

This section answers:

“Why choose THIS over competitors?”

⸻

5. Common Use Cases

Very powerful for advisers.

Examples:

Scenario    Positioning
New parents Income replacement
Property purchase   Mortgage protection
High-income professional    Tax-efficient legacy
Young executive Starter CI coverage

This helps newer agents recommend naturally.

⸻

6. Key Limitations / Objections

Extremely important internally.

Agents should know:

* when NOT to recommend
* common client objections
* weaknesses vs competitors

Examples:

* Higher premiums after age 45
* Weak surrender value early years
* No premium offset option
* Stricter underwriting
* No early CI coverage
* Investment risk applies

This prevents mis-selling and improves credibility.

⸻

7. Underwriting Notes

Agents LOVE this section.

Examples:

* Simplified underwriting available
* Strict for diabetes cases
* Better acceptance for hypertension
* BMI limits
* Medical exam threshold
* Foreigners eligible?

This dramatically improves field efficiency.

⸻

8. Rider Compatibility

Very useful for cross-selling.

Rider   Available?
Early CI rider  Yes
Hospital rider  No
Disability waiver   Yes
Payor benefit   Yes

Could also include:

* most commonly paired riders
* recommended bundles

⸻

9. Competitor Comparison (Optional but Powerful)

Simple internal positioning.

Product Better For  Competitor Better For
Lower premiums  Higher cash value
Better CI flexibility   Better multiplier

This helps advisers avoid confusion between similar plans.

⸻

10. Compliance / Advisory Notes

Critical for management.

Examples:

* Must explain non-guaranteed returns
* ILP investment risks disclosure required
* Not suitable for short-term holding
* Needs risk profiling
* CPFIS eligibility restrictions

This reduces compliance exposure.

⸻

11. Quick Numbers Section

Agents often need “ballpark figures.”

Include:

* sample premiums
* entry ages
* minimum premium
* policy term range
* maximum coverage
* multiplier duration

Example:

Item    Value
Entry age   18–70
Min premium $50/month
Term options    5–40 years
Max sum assured $5
```
